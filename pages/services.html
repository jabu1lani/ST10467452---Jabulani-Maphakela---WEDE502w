Our Services

Community Connect offers a variety of programs and services designed to support community members and strengthen our neighborhood.

Educational & Workshop Services

  • Gardening 101 Workshops: Series covering soil health, composting, seed starting, planting schedules, and organic pest control.
  • Youth Gardening Programs: After-school or summer programs where kids learn to plant, grow, and harvest their own food.
  • Composting & Soil Health Clinics: Hands-on lessons in building compost bins, worm farming (vermicomposting), and understanding soil science.

Community-Building & Wellness

  • Community Work Days: Organized events where volunteers come together for large tasks (building new beds, spreading mulch, harvesting for donations) followed by a shared meal.
  • Skill-Sharing Library: A small shed or board where gardeners can borrow tools and exchange knowledge, seeds, and recipes.

Food Access & Sustainability

  • Donation Plots: Dedicating a portion of the garden specifically to grow fresh produce for local food banks, shelters, and soup kitchens.
  • Seed & Seedling Swap/Sale: An annual event in the spring where gardeners exchange or purchase heirloom and organic seeds and starter plants.

Specialized & Advanced Gardening Services

  • Herb Garden & Medicinal Plant Workshops: Maintaining an herb spiral and teaching about growing and using culinary and medicinal herbs.
  • Season Extension Workshops: Teaching techniques like using cold frames, hoop houses, and row covers to grow food earlier in the spring and later into autumn.

How to Access Services

Most of our services are available to community members at no cost. For information about eligibility or to get connected with services, please contact us or visit our community center during open hours.
